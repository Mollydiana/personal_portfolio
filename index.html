I grew up in a family of musicians. I only dabbled in music until my early 20’s, when my father persuaded my brother and me to come with him as his backup band on a tour of Galicia (the celtic province in northwest Spain).



Although we were only supposed to be there for two weeks, I ended up staying for nearly three years. While I was there, I began to seriously play the mandolin, the fiddle and the accordion and was blessed to inherit a repertoire of songs and melodies that have been passed on for generations. I helped form a band and recorded a CD of traditional music. I brought the band back to the U.S. where we toured the West Coast, introducing many people to the wonderful and unique spirit of Galician culture.



In 2012 I recorded an albumn with my mother, father and brother entitled 'The Family Carr'. On it we played traditional songs from Galicia, Ireland, Appalachia and Quebec.








 

While living in Galicia from 2008-2011, I became more and more fascinated with its rich culture and language. I learned Spanish and Galego, the native Galician language. I became so fascinated by the peoples’ struggle to preserve their language and culture, that I made this short documentary so that I could share their story.



The study of and teaching of languages nurtured my fascination with communication in general. I found myself drawn to different ways of communicating ideas artfully. At a certain point my interests grew to include computer languages and design.
