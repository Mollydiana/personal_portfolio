I grew up in a family of musicians. I only dabbled in music until my early 20â€™s, when my father persuaded my brother and me to come with him as his backup band on a tour of Galicia (the celtic province in northwest Spain).



Although we were only supposed to be there for two weeks, I ended up staying for nearly three years. While I was there, I began to seriously play the mandolin, the fiddle and the accordion and was blessed to inherit a repertoire of songs and melodies that have been passed on for generations. I helped form a band and recorded a CD of traditional music. I brought the band back to the U.S. where we toured the West Coast, introducing many people to the wonderful and unique spirit of Galician culture.



In 2012 I recorded an albumn with my mother, father and brother entitled 'The Family Carr'. On it we played traditional songs from Galicia, Ireland, Appalachia and Quebec.
